High Risk Counties Related to Opioid Prescribing¶

by Chi Zhang¶

Investigation Overview¶

Investigate the risk level of counties through Opioid use and prescribing

Dataset Overview¶

There are 78 attributes and 12884 complete rows in the dataset. All variables are numeric execpt for year, FIPS, and two target variables that are binary. The main features of interest can be target_LAOPR_80,Long_Acting_Opioid_Pres2 and Opioid_Prescribing_Rate2 which all indicate the risk level of counties in terms of Opioid use and prescribing.

Distributions of Beneficiaries in MA and FFS Programs¶

The log transformed distribution of the two shows that MA is more spread out with a peak around 10, whereas FFS has a peak around 30. FFS being on the right of MA shows that there are more beneficiaries enrolled in FFS than MA.

Compare Long acting and overall Opioid Rates against Poverty Rates¶

Compared the two graphs, long acting opioid rates decrease as poverty rate increases, but the overall opioid rates do not.

Net Migration vs. Long Acting Opioid Prescribing Rates¶

The graph between positive net migration and long acting rate shows that counties with large number of migrations tend to have long acting rates concentrated around 15.

Risk Accessment between MA and FFS Beneficiaries¶

Though There is no clear correlation between Long Acting Rates and number of beneficiaries; over the years, FFS benes in high risk counties have been increasing to catch up to low risk counties. MA benes on the other hand, shows a gap between high risk and low risk counties, especially in 2016 and 2017.
